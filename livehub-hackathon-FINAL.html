How to Enter

Two things.
That's it.

No GitHub. No slide deck. Just an agent that works and a video that sells it.

🎤

Core Rule: Voice First. Always.

Every submission must be a callable, working voice AI agent built on LiveHub. You provide a DID (Direct Inward Dialing phone number) or WebRTC link. Judges will call it. It must pick up, converse intelligently, and handle your chosen use case from start to finish.

1

Pick a use case you believe in

Any real-world scenario. The only rule: you should be proud to say "I built that."

2

Build a LiveHub AI Agent that handles it

Configure your agent so it handles the full conversation correctly. Judges will call your DID number and test it end to end.

3

Record a 60-second video — however you want

Selfie cam. Screen recording. Full AI commercial. No rules. Be creative or don't. Totally up to you.

4

Submit both before midnight June 23

Your DID number with brief test instructions, and your 60-second video. That's the whole submission.

What is a DID? A Direct Inward Dialing number is a real, callable phone number assigned to your LiveHub AI agent. When someone dials it, your agent answers. You get one when you set up your agent in LiveHub — submit that number so judges can call and test you directly.

Prompt Tips

Writing a great agent prompt

The quality of your prompt is the quality of your agent.

Basic prompt structure

### Identity
You are [name], a friendly voice agent
for [company] [department].

### Context
[Background about the product/service]

### Objective
- [Task 1] / - [Task 2]

### Instructions
1. Ask the caller for their name.
2. Determine their intent.
3. Use the get_slots tool.

### Format
Keep answers short.
Never use HTML or markdown.
You communicate through speech.

Voice-specific best practices

1. Always declare voice mode:
"You communicate through speech.
Avoid long responses, never use
HTML or markdown."

2. Use numbered steps:
"Follow steps in order. Ask up to
one question per response."

3. Be explicit about tools:
"Use get_appointments with
date set to today's date."

4. For branching:
"If caller says yes, go to step 3.
If no, end the call politely."
